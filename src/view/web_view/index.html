How to read the result
The analyzer fetches /robots.txt from the URL and runs three checks:

1. Tier (RQ1) -- assigns a defense tier 1-5 based on which bot layers are blocked and how.

2. Conflicts (RQ2) -- detects five classes of directive contradictions that make protection semantically ineffective despite its appearance.

3. Compliance score (RQ3) -- weighted 0.0-1.0 across three bot layers: APP layer 35%, INFRA layer 45%, Google AI 20%. A site blocking only GPTBot scores 0.35, not 1.0.

Thesis context
About this Tool

What this thesis does

This tool is the artifact of the bachelor thesis "Semantic Configuration Analysis of robots.txt for EU AI Act Compliance" (2DV50E, Linnaeus University, VT 2026). It investigates whether European news publishers are effectively protecting their content from AI training data crawlers using the Robots Exclusion Protocol.

Most existing tools check robots.txt in a binary way -- a bot is either blocked or it is not. This tool performs semantic analysis: it checks whether the configuration is internally consistent under RFC 9309 parsing rules and whether the protection actually holds, not just on the surface.

The EU AI Act (Recital 105 / Article 53(1)(c)) requires GPAI model providers to respect machine-readable opt-outs. robots.txt is the primary such mechanism. This tool measures how many publishers have a valid, effective opt-out.

What the results mean

Compliance status
COMPLIANTAll three bot layers blocked without internal contradictions. Valid machine-readable opt-out under EU AI Act Recital 105. AI training crawlers are legally required to respect this.
NOMINALThe site names AI bots (showing intent) but HIGH severity conflicts make blocking semantically ineffective. The Enumeration Fallacy -- the site believes it is protected but is not. Binary tools would count this as compliant; the SCA does not.
PARTIALSome layers blocked, not all. Score between 0.01 and 0.99. Partial compliance gap under Article 53(1)(c).
NON_COMPLIANTNo effective AI opt-out. All AI training collectors have full access. No reservation of rights exists under EU AI Act Recital 105.
Compliance score (0.0 to 1.0)

A weighted score across three crawling layers. Blocking only ChatGPT/ClaudeBot but not CCBot scores 0.35 -- it blocks the visible bots but misses the infrastructure collectors responsible for most training data harvesting. Score 1.0 means all three layers effectively blocked.

Conflict types
WILDCARD_OVERRIDE
HIGH		A wildcard Allow: / re-grants access to specifically blocked bots. The core mechanism of the Enumeration Fallacy. Most dangerous conflict type.
DUPLICATE_SECTION
MEDIUM		Same User-agent declared in multiple sections. RFC 9309 requires merging but non-compliant crawlers may read only one section, potentially the permissive one.
ALLOW_DISALLOW
HIGH		Same path in both Allow and Disallow for one agent. RFC 9309 says Allow wins -- contrary to blocking intent.
ORDERING_VIOLATION
LOW		Disallow: / combined with specific Allow rules. RFC uses specificity not order. Operators expecting .htaccess behavior are surprised.
EMPTY_DISALLOW
HIGH		Empty Disallow: (means allow all) mixed with real blocking rules. Nullifies intent in RFC-compliant parsers.

How this differs from other tools

Existing binary parsers

Standard tools check whether a specific bot appears with a Disallow directive. They answer "Is GPTBot blocked?" with yes or no. They do not check whether the block is semantically effective, whether other directives cancel it, or whether the INFRA layer is also covered.

This tool -- semantic analysis

The SCA parses robots.txt into a structured section map and evaluates it as a semantic unit. It checks all five conflict classes, evaluates three distinct bot layers with weighted importance, and distinguishes between intended and effective protection. A site can appear protected while being fully open.

Prior academic studies

Liu et al. (2025), Cui et al. (2025), and Dinzinger et al. (2024) measured robots.txt adoption using binary parsers. They report "X% block GPTBot" without checking whether that block is actually effective or undermined by conflicting directives in the same file.

This thesis contribution

By introducing the Enumeration Fallacy and the six-tier Defense taxonomy, this thesis shows that binary measurement overstates compliance. The NOMINAL category identifies sites that binary tools mark as protected but the SCA reveals as vulnerable -- a gap invisible to all prior work.
